Chapter 2 · Real-life meaning

A cover amount only matters if it helps hold real life together.

Most people do not care about abstract leverage. They care about what happens to a household if life is suddenly disrupted.

Keep the home stable

Rent, mortgage, and essential bills are less likely to become a second crisis immediately.

Protect routine living costs

Food, transport, childcare, and ordinary living costs still need to be carried by someone.

Reduce pressure on a partner

The full impact does not automatically collapse onto one person to carry alone.

Create time to recover

Protection does not remove loss, but it can create more room to manage disruption and adjust.
