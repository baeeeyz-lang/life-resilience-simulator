Chapter 2 · Real-life meaning

What could a lump sum payout mean for your family?

If you have life cover and die during your policy term, your family receives a lump sum. How they use it is their choice — but here are some of the things it could help with.

Pay off the mortgage or rent

A payout could be used to settle an outstanding mortgage or cover rent, helping your family stay in their home without that financial pressure falling on them.

Support day-to-day living costs

Without your income, everyday costs — food, bills, childcare, transport — still need to be met. A lump sum can help bridge that gap while your family adjusts.

Settle outstanding loans or debts

Life cover can also help clear other financial obligations — such as personal loans or outstanding debts — so your family is not left managing these alone.

Create space to make considered decisions

A payout cannot replace what has been lost. But having financial breathing room can allow your family the time they need to grieve, adjust, and plan for the future — without immediate pressure.
