Chapter 2 · Real-life meaning

What could a lump sum payout mean for your family?

If you have life cover and die during your policy term, your family receives a lump sum. How they use it is their choice — but here are some of the things it could help with.

Pay off the mortgage or rent

A payout could be used to settle an outstanding mortgage or cover rent, helping your family stay in their home without that financial pressure falling on them.

Support day-to-day living costs

Without your income, everyday costs — food, bills, childcare, transport — still need to be met. A lump sum can help bridge that gap while your family adjusts.

Settle outstanding loans or debts

Life cover can also help clear other financial obligations — such as personal loans or outstanding debts — so your family is not left managing these alone.

Create space to make considered decisions

A payout cannot replace what has been lost. But having financial breathing room can allow your family the time they need to grieve, adjust, and plan for the future — without immediate pressure.

Chapter 3 · Claim journey

If something serious happens,
what could the journey involve?

The aim here is not to promise a perfect experience. It is to make the part of the journey that people fear most feel less uncertain — by showing what actually happens, step by step.

1

Trigger & Notify

INITIAL CONTACT

For illness-related claims, you can notify us by completing an online claim form or by calling our specialist claims team. For life cover claims following a bereavement, a family member can contact us via our dedicated bereavement support page. The first step is to establish what has happened and what you need to do next.

2

Evidence Gathering

VERIFICATION

Depending on your claim type, you will need to provide specific documents. These typically include your policy number, details of your diagnosis, and your GP or hospital specialist's contact details. Copies of medical notes or correspondence are also helpful and can speed up the process. We will contact your GP or consultant directly if additional information is needed.

3

Assessment & Review

DEEP REVIEW

Your claim is reviewed by a dedicated claims team, who check it against your policy terms and the evidence provided. You will receive updates on progress throughout this stage, so you always know where things stand.

4

The Decision

OUTCOME

Once the assessment is complete, you will be informed of the outcome clearly. Scottish Widows paid 99% of life cover claims in 2024. If a claim is not approved, the reason will be explained to you.

5

Payout & Relief

COMPLETION

Approved claims are paid as promptly as possible. Life insurance and critical illness cover pay out as a lump sum. Income protection provides regular monthly payments for the duration agreed under your policy. Payment is made to the nominated beneficiary or bank account.

6

Ongoing Support

POST-CLAIM

Throughout the process, we will keep you updated on the progress of your claim. You can contact us at any time with questions. If you hold a Scottish Widows Protect policy, you also have access to Scottish Widows Care, which provides practical and emotional support through RedArc — at no additional cost.
